Introduction

To check whether the gene expression of OSMR and/or LIFR differ, we performed two types of logistic regression, and additionaly performed differential expression analysis for the entire gene set used, from which we can then filter OSMR/LIFR.

Interpreting statistics
When assessing above statistical approach, we can conclude the following: there are possible differences between OSMR expression values between consecutive stability scores, based on p-values (three below 0.05), but none based on CIs (CI crosses zero).
The THREE p-values that are significant are the comparison between score 0 and 1, between score 2 and 3, and between score 3 and 4. Looking at the graph, these indeed seem the likeliest to differ, although the differences seem to be minimal.

Note however, that for ordinal logistic regression the assumption is that the relationship between each pair of outcome groups is the same. This is called the proportional odds assumption. When assessing this, Im not so sure whether the data fits this assumption.

An alternative approach is using multinomial logistic regression for stability, assuming that the categoris of the outcome variable (plaque stability) are not ordered.

Interpreting statistics
When looking at the above statistics, in the end the p-values are in the last column. These are p-values for the comparison of OSMR expression values of one of the row denominators (1, 2, 3 or 4, i.e.scores 1-4) and expression values of our baseline stability score 0.

Although non are significant (<0.05), they follow the general trend that we saw in the boxplot for OSMR expression vs.stability scores (see figure 1, left panel).
